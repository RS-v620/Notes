What if it came down to just this moment? Whatever it contains for you right now. Whatever life is showing you this very instant. What if this is all you really had to attend to?

Try it for a moment. If it is stress you are feeling, let it be there. Just take a look at it. See what it feels like in your body. If it's boredom you are feeling, notice the simple fact of it. Here you are, in this moment, kind of uninspired. If it's happiness you are feeling, see if you can track an expression on your face. Without even looking in the mirror. Are your lips open a little? Pursed ? Scrunched up to one side in a smirk ? What else does this moment contain? Are you breathing indoor air or outdoor air? Is there a breeze? How do you know? Is it your hair that moves? Or a sensation on your face? Is it hot? Cold? Warm? Freezing? Is there a scent? Can you just notice? There is no need to think about it. Just pay attention to it like you are tuning towards a friend. 

When you indulge in moments like these, you are actually joining a great traditions across many centuries. The historian Pierre Hadot tells us that Socrates had no actual system to teach. "Throughout, his philosophy was a spiritual exercise, an invitation to a new way of life, active reflection and living consciousness."

And so, two thousand years later, that invitation has landed on your doorstep. An invitation to a new way of life. A life of active reflection...noticing the colours in the sky. Noticing your emotions as they change throughout the day. The invitation is not just to a conscious-life but to living consciousness!

You get to reach into sunlit waters of your life as you glide like a manta ray ... or dart like schools of fish through kelp forests swaying back and forth. Keep noticing.

You are real, and you are part of this world around you. 

By remaining aware, your consciousness itself can be alive.
