The author Elizabeth Gilbert illustrates her creative process with the term "Big Magic". She describes the importance of honoring her inspiration as passionately as one would indulge in a love affair. She applauds arrogance saying, "No one is going to give you 'permission slip' to be a creator". There is a wonderful old saying, 'Seek forgiveness, not permission'. You must  believe that you are entitled to create, no matter the number of rejections or setbacks you face.

You can actually dance quite merrily across the landscape of rejection. Image not taking it personally, of-course that is more easier said than done. But let's just go with this notion for a moment. What if you put fear in the back seat instead of the driver's seat on every creative journey you take. It's still on board. It might even try to backseat-drive or pester you with endless "are we there yet?" like a small child would on a road trip. What if failing just mean moving on from a project and trusting that the next one will come to take its place. 

The world is chaotic. It is a spectacularly beautiful mess of happenstance and luck that mixes with your hard-work and effort. Ask yourself if despair or anxiety are getting in your way. Then ask yourself if you can try thinking of your so called failures simply as mulch. What if every past attempt actually nourishes everything you do in unseen ways. Find the delight again. Those illicit moments you sneak away to meet your muse. Those sparks of inspiration and "aha" moments. Recreate that relationship with your creative process, as if you found an old friend. Pay attention to it and it won't be able to help but pay attention to you
